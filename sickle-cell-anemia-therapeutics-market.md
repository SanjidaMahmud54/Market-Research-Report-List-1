<p><p>The sickle-cell anemia therapeutics market encompasses various treatment modalities including blood transfusion, pharmacotherapy, and bone marrow transplant. Blood transfusions are often used to manage symptoms and prevent complications by providing healthy red blood cells. Pharmacotherapy includes medications like hydroxyurea that aim to reduce pain crises and improve quality of life. Bone marrow transplants can potentially cure the disease by replacing defective blood-forming cells with healthy ones, though this option is often limited by donor availability and patient's health status.</p></p>
