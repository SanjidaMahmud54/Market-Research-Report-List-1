<p><ul><li>Anionic Surfactant</li><li>Cationic Surfactant</li><li>Amphoteric Surfactant</li><li>Nonionic Surfactant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The specialty surfactants market comprises four main types: anionic, cationic, amphoteric, and nonionic surfactants. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge and are commonly used in detergents and personal care products due to their excellent cleaning properties. Cationic surfactants possess a positive charge, often used as fabric softeners and antimicrobial agents. Amphoteric surfactants can carry both charges, offering versatility in formulations. Nonionic surfactants lack any charge, making them ideal for applications where mildness and compatibility with various ingredients are essential.</p></p>
