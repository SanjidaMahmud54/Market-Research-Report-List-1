<p><p>The Near-infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS) market is primarily segmented into two types: Fourier Transform Near-Infrared (FT-NIR) and UV-Vis-NIR spectrometers. FT-NIR utilizes a Fourier transform technique to provide high-resolution spectral data and is commonly used in pharmaceutical and agricultural applications for quality control. In contrast, UV-Vis-NIR spectrometers operate across a broader wavelength range, including ultraviolet to near-infrared light, facilitating various applications in environmental monitoring, food quality analysis, and chemical identification, thereby catering to diverse industry needs.</p></p>
