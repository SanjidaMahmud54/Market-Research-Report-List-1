<p><p>The wood-derived food additives market encompasses several key types. Fibrillated cellulose acts as a thickener and stabilizer, enhancing texture in various products. Lignin serves as a natural preservative and antioxidant, improving shelf life. Xylan is a carbohydrate that aids in moisture retention and can be used as a low-calorie sweetener. Vanillin, derived from lignin or guaiacol, provides a natural flavoring agent, enhancing the taste profile of food products. Collectively, these additives improve food quality and sustainability.</p></p>
