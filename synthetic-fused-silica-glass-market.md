<p><ul><li>CVD Process</li><li>Plasma Deposition Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The synthetic fused silica glass market primarily consists of two key production processes: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Plasma Deposition. The CVD process involves creating silica through gas-phase reactions that deposit material onto a substrate, resulting in high purity and homogeneity. In contrast, the Plasma Deposition process utilizes ionized gases at high temperatures to form a silica coating, offering improved adhesion and uniformity. Both methods are crucial for industries like optics, telecommunications, and semiconductor manufacturing, fulfilling their high-performance material needs.</p></p>
