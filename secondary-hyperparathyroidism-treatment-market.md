<p><p>The secondary hyperparathyroidism treatment market encompasses several key types. Surgery involves the removal of hyperplastic parathyroid tissue to alleviate symptoms. Drugs specifically targeting parathyroid hormone levels are also utilized. Vitamin D supplements help manage calcium levels, reducing parathyroid activity. Calcimimetics mimic calcium to lower parathyroid hormone secretion, providing a non-surgical option. Phosphate binders are used to control phosphate levels in patients with kidney disease, indirectly reducing parathyroid stimulation. Together, these treatments aim to manage and mitigate the effects of the condition.</p></p>
