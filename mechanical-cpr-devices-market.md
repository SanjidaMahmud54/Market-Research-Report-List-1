<p><ul><li>Electric</li><li>Pneumatic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mechanical CPR devices are categorized into electric and pneumatic types, both designed to enhance cardiopulmonary resuscitation efficiency. Electric devices utilize battery-powered mechanisms to provide consistent compressions, ensuring optimal depth and rate while reducing rescuer fatigue. Pneumatic devices, on the other hand, operate using compressed air to achieve similar results. Both types are essential in emergency medical situations, as they allow for continuous CPR, improving patient outcomes during cardiac arrest scenarios by delivering precise compressions effectively and consistently.</p></p>
