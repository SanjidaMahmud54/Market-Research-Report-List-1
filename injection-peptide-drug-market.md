<p><ul><li>Intravenous Injection</li><li>Subcutaneous Injection</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The injection peptide drug market is primarily segmented into two types: intravenous (IV) and subcutaneous (SC) injections. Intravenous injection delivers peptides directly into the bloodstream, providing rapid effects and precise control over dosage, making it suitable for urgent treatments. In contrast, subcutaneous injection involves administering peptides into the fatty tissue just beneath the skin, offering ease of use and convenience for patients, especially for chronic conditions. Each method caters to different therapeutic needs and patient preferences within the peptide drug sector.</p></p>
