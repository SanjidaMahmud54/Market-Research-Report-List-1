<p><p>The blood substitute market primarily consists of two types: Hemoglobin-based oxygen carriers (HBOCs) and perfluorocarbon-based oxygen carriers (PFBOCs). HBOCs are derived from hemoglobin and are designed to transport oxygen, mimicking red blood cells. PFBOCs, on the other hand, utilize perfluorocarbons to dissolve and carry oxygen more effectively. Both types aim to address blood supply shortages and are developed for various medical applications, including trauma care and surgical procedures, enhancing oxygen delivery in critical situations.</p></p>
