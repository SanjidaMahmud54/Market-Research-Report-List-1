Wired routers connect devices directly using Ethernet cables, offering stable and high-speed connections, making them suitable for environments with high data demands. In contrast, wireless routers provide the convenience of mobility and connectivity without cables, utilizing Wi-Fi technology to connect multiple devices within a range. Both types have distinct advantages, catering to different user needs, including residential, commercial, and industrial applications.</p></p>
