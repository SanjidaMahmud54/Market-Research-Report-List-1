<p><p>The microbial biosurfactants market comprises several types, including rhamnolipids, sophorolipids, and others. Rhamnolipids, produced primarily by Pseudomonas species, are known for their effective surface-active properties and applications in bioremediation and pharmaceuticals. Sophorolipids, derived from yeast species like Candida, are used in cosmetics and cleaning products due to their low toxicity and biodegradability. The "others" category includes diverse biosurfactants such as mannosylerythritol lipids and trehalose lipids, each with unique functionalities and applications across various industries.</p></p>
