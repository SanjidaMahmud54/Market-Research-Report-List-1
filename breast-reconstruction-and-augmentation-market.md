<p><p>Breast reconstruction and augmentation encompass two primary market types: implant reconstruction and autologous reconstruction. Implant reconstruction involves the use of silicone or saline implants to restore breast shape after mastectomy or for cosmetic enhancement. In contrast, autologous reconstruction utilizes the patient's own tissue, typically harvested from areas like the abdomen or back, to recreate the breast. Both methods cater to different aesthetic and medical needs, with varying recovery times, risks, and results tailored to individual preferences.</p></p>
