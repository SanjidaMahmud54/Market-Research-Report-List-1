<p><p>The water hardness removal market comprises various types, including salt-based and salt-free methods. Salt-based water hardness removal typically involves ion exchange systems, using sodium ions to displace calcium and magnesium ions, effectively softening the water. Salt-free methods, such as template-assisted crystallization, reduce scale formation without adding sodium, appealing to health-conscious consumers. Other techniques may include physical filtration and reverse osmosis. Each method addresses water hardness with distinct technologies catering to diverse consumer needs and environmental considerations.</p></p>
