<p><p>The phototherapy apparatus market includes three primary types: conventional, LED, and fiber optic systems. Conventional phototherapy uses fluorescent or halogen lamps for treatment, delivering broad-spectrum light to infants with jaundice. LED phototherapy utilizes light-emitting diodes, offering targeted wavelengths and energy efficiency, which leads to quicker treatment times and reduced heat exposure. Fiber optic systems use illuminated fibers to provide light therapy, enabling more precise application and direct treatment to affected areas. Each type serves various clinical needs in neonatal care.</p></p>
