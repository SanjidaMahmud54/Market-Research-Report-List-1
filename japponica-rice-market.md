<p><p>Japonica rice, a medium-grain variety, is categorized into translucent and opaque rice markets. Translucent rice, often preferred for sushi and premium dishes, has a clear, glassy appearance due to higher amylose content, offering a firmer texture when cooked. Opaque rice, with a cloudy or chalky look, contains more amylopectin, making it softer and stickier, ideal for dishes like risotto or desserts. Both types cater to distinct culinary preferences, influencing their demand and pricing in regional and global markets.</p></p>
