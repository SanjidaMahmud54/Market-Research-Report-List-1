<p><p>Hot swap markets can be categorized into high voltage, low voltage, and PCI hot swap. High voltage hot swap involves replacing components in systems with voltages exceeding 1,000V, ensuring safety and operation continuity. Low voltage hot swap, typically under 1,000V, is common in consumer electronics, allowing quick component replacement without system downtime. PCI hot swap focuses on the PCI bus architecture in computing, enabling the addition or removal of expansion cards without shutting down the system, enhancing flexibility and maintenance.</p></p>
