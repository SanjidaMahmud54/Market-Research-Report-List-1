<p><p>Devic's Syndrome, or Neuromyelitis Optica, is treated using several key medications. Eculizumab, a monoclonal antibody, targets complement protein to reduce relapses. Methylprednisolone is often used for acute exacerbations, providing rapid immunosuppression. Azathioprine and Mycophenolate Mofetil serve as long-term immunomodulatory therapies to prevent future attacks. Rituximab, another monoclonal antibody, depletes CD20+ B-cells, offering a targeted approach to manage active disease. Collectively, these treatments aim to improve patient outcomes and reduce the frequency of attacks.</p></p>
