<p><ul><li>Lithium-Based Battery</li><li>Nickel-Based Battery</li><li>Lead Acid Battery</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The commercial aircraft battery market consists of three primary types: lithium-based, nickel-based, and lead-acid batteries. Lithium-based batteries are favored for their high energy density and lightweight properties, making them ideal for modern aircraft systems. Nickel-based batteries, while heavier, offer good performance in cold temperatures and longevity. Lead-acid batteries are the most traditional option, known for their reliability and cost-effectiveness, though they are heavier and have lower energy density compared to newer technologies. Each type serves specific operational needs in aviation.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Main Battery</li><li>APU Battery</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The commercial aircraft battery market primarily includes two key applications: main batteries and auxiliary power unit (APU) batteries. APU batteries support auxiliary power units, which supply energy for cabin heating, cooling, and electrical loads when the main engines are not running. Both battery types are crucial for enhancing aircraft reliability, efficiency, and overall performance in modern aviation operations.</p></p>
